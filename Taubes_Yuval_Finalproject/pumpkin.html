How to Carve Pumpkin

In the following article, you will learn how to carve a Jack-O-Lantern, the staple image of Halloween. With just a few tools, and some time, your Jack-O-Lantern is going to look super spooky.

The first step to carving a Jack-O-lantern is to get a pumpkin. You can do this many ways, however, we recommend going to your local pumpkin patch for the best pumpkins. get a pumpkin around the size of a basketball. For the weight of the pumpkin, you want to avoid the super heavy ones, due to the extra work needed to extract its guts.

The right size for your pumpkin. Image Source: Stocksy

Once you have the perfect pumpkin it's time to get your tools. The tools we recommend for cutting a pumpkin are:

  • Marker
  • Big Knife
  • Small Knife
  • spoon
We are going to use the marker for drawing our design, the big knife to cut out the lid, the small knife to cut our design, and the spoon to scoop out the seeds and pulp.

It’s time to cut; place the pumpkin on a flat even surface, so the pumpkin is stable. Take your marker and draw a circle around the stem of the pumpkin. Now take your big knife and place it at a 45-degree angle relative to the pumpkin. Once in position, start cutting the pumpkin until you complete your circle.

How you want to cut your pumpkin. Image Source: Wikihow

Once complete the cutting circle pull the knife out. Once the knife is out grab the stem of the pumpkin and pull. If the cutting was done correctly the lid of the pumpkin will come right out. Make sure to save this piece of the pumpkin.

How to pull the lid out. Image Source: Wikihow

Once the lid of the pumpkin has been pulled out, it’s time to start scooping. Grab your spoon and pull the guts of the pumpkin out. If you want to save the seeds of the pumpkin now is the time to separate it from the pulp and put it aside, otherwise dispose of it.

Cleaning out the insides of a pumpkin. Image source: Better Homes and gardens

It’s now time to sketch your design on the pumpkin. We would recommend starting with something simple like triangles for eyes and a spooky grin. Once you have your chosen design draw it onto the pumpkin face with the marker.

Sketching the face of the Jack-O-lantern. Image Source: Instructables

It is now time to cut your Jack-O-Lantern. Take your small knife and start slowly cutting out your sketch, if the pumpkin is too thick then grab your bigger knife. While doing this make sure the pumpkin is on an even, stable surface.

Cutting the Pumpkin. Image Source: Instructables

Now it’s time for the lighting, with either a real candle or just an electric alternative. Once you have chosen your lighting, open the lid of your pumpkin and place it inside. If you are using a candle make sure the candle is on an even stable surface, to avoid a fire. With your light inside, close your lid.

A lit Jack-O-Lantern. Image source: Eckerts

Congratulation! You have now finished creating your Jack-O-Lantern. Don’t worry if it’s not perfect yet, all you need is more practice. You are now prepared for the spookiest time of the year.
